History of Ecmascript

Due to the rapid growth of JavaScript, it became evident in 1997 that the language would need to be properly maintained and managed. As a result, Netscape delegated the task of developing a language definition to the European Computer Manufacturers Association (ECMA), an organization dedicated to computer standardization. ECMA-262 was the name of the ECMA standards, and ECMAScript languages comprised JavaScript, JScript, and ActionScript. Between 1997 and 1999, three revisions of ECMA-262 were released, but version 4 was dropped over a decade later due to disagreements over the language's direction and proposed enhancements.

What is Ecmascript?

ECMAScript is a JavaScript-based scripting language developed by the European Computer Manufacturers Association. In 1996, Microsoft developed its own scripting languages, such as VBScript and JScript. EcmaScript was created by Ecma International after Netscape submitted JavaScript for standardization in November 1996. EcmaScript was also included in the ECMA-262 standard's first edition, which was published in June 1997. Despite their similarities, JavaScript and EcmaScript are not interchangeable.
